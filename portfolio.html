Gallery

All of the photos here were captured by me during my time in Oklahoma and upon my return to California. Each represent a special memory or meaning.

After reading David Gran’s book: Killers of the Flower Moon, I was inspired to explore the area in Oklahoma where the story took place. Not too far from my home, I often drove up to Osage County and explored rural and historical areas on the Osage Reservation.

Is it possible to say no to a butterfly kite?

I love chickens!

Driving out to a friends place for 4th of July, I spotted this red heifer that had clearly jumped out of her pasture. She was enjoying the tall Johnson grass. Her beautiful red coat and lush green grass made for the perfect photo opportunity.

For 5-years, I passed this tree everyday on my way to and from work. I always looked forward to see it. A top a small hill, it was clearly the only tree in this pasture. On hot summer days or on cold snowy days, there would be a herd of cattle seeking its shelter.
